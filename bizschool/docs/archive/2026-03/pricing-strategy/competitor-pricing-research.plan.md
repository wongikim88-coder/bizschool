## 1. Overview

### 1.1 Purpose

Establish a fact-based pricing framework for BIZSCHOOL's combined service model (online courses, book sales, expert consultation, offline courses) by analyzing real competitor pricing data collected in March 2026.

### 1.2 Background

BIZSCHOOL operates at the intersection of three service categories that are typically sold separately in Korea: online/offline education, content (books), and professional expert consultation. To set competitive and sustainable prices, we need to understand how comparable platforms price each service type, and whether bundled or subscription models are gaining traction.

This document consolidates research gathered via live web searches in March 2026.

### 3.2 Pricing Model Patterns

Three dominant models are observed:

1. **Pure Subscription** (CLASS101+, Millie, Welaaa, all international platforms): Annual subscription unlocking unlimited access. Range: 119,000–575,000 KRW/year for Korean platforms.

2. **Marketplace / Per-Transaction** (Taling, Kmong, Inflearn): Per-course or per-service purchase. No subscription lock-in. High flexibility, variable revenue.

3. **Hybrid — Subscription + Per-Course** (FastCampus, Douzone): All-access pass for regular users + individual course purchase option.

### 3.3 Consultation Pricing Benchmarks

The Korean market for online expert consultation is thin and fragmented. Key data points:
- Very basic Q&A / quick consultation: 4,000–10,000 KRW
- Standard 30-min professional consultation: 30,000–55,000 KRW
- 1-hour full consulting session: 55,000–110,000 KRW
- Monthly retainer (bookkeeping): 100,000–150,000 KRW

## 6. Strategic Implications (Actionable Insights)

### 6.1 Recommended Pricing Approach for BIZSCHOOL

Based on the research, the following pricing framework is recommended for team discussion:

**Tier A — Individual Purchase (No Commitment)**
- Online courses: 30,000–150,000 KRW per course (competitive with Inflearn/Taling)
- Books: Market price (publisher-set)
- Expert consultation: 30,000–55,000 KRW per session (competitive with Kmong)
- Offline courses: 100,000–400,000 KRW per course (competitive with Samil/Taling)

**Tier B — Monthly Subscription (Mid-Commitment)**
- Courses + Consultation access bundle: 29,900–49,900 KRW/month
- Benchmark: Welaaa (12,500 KRW/month for content only) is the floor; FastCampus (90,000–165,000 KRW/month for courses only) is a high-end reference
- Adding consultation access justifies a premium above content-only platforms

**Tier C — Annual Subscription (High Value)**
- All-access (courses + books + consultation sessions included): 199,000–399,000 KRW/year
- Benchmarks: CLASS101+ (199,000–324,000 KRW/year for courses only); Coursera Plus (~575,000 KRW/year for courses only)
- BIZSCHOOL's multi-service bundle should price above CLASS101+ but offer broader value

### 6.2 Positioning Recommendation

BIZSCHOOL should position as a **"Professional Knowledge + Action" platform**:
- Not a creative hobby platform (vs. CLASS101)
- Not a pure IT/coding platform (vs. Inflearn/FastCampus)
- Not a commodity consulting marketplace (vs. Kmong)
- Closer to: Welaaa (content + learning bundle) + Taling (practical skill instruction) + expert consultation

The sweet spot is **business/professional upskilling with integrated expert access** — a market gap confirmed by the competitive matrix.

### 6.3 Risks

| Risk | Impact | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|--------|------------|------------|
| Subscription fatigue among Korean consumers | Medium | Medium | Offer flexible per-transaction option alongside subscription |
| Price pressure from Inflearn/CLASS101 expanding into adjacent services | High | Medium | Differentiate through consultation quality and offline integration |
| Low willingness-to-pay for bundled services vs. individual | Medium | High | Test pricing tiers with small user cohort before full launch |
| Samil PwC Academy entering consumer market | Low | Low | Monitor quarterly |
